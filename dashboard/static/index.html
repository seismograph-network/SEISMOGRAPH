What is this?

Plain-language guide

SEISMOGRAPH is a privacy-preserving drift detector for LLM APIs. It runs small, fixed “canary” prompts against a model on a schedule and watches whether the model’s behaviour quietly changes over time.

Why it matters

A provider can silently update a model — same name, different behaviour — with no announcement. That can break your prompts overnight. SEISMOGRAPH tells you when it happens, so you know it’s the model, not you.

How to read a card

Each card is one model. The coloured dot and badge show its current status; the numbers are recent behaviour (average answer length and JSON success rate) — privacy-noised aggregates, never raw text.

STABLE — the model is behaving in line with its established baseline. Nothing unusual; no confirmed drift.
DRIFTING — several independent probes agree the model’s behaviour has shifted from its baseline. A single probe is never enough to raise this — worth investigating before you rely on its output.
